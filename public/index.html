School and summer coding camps!

Summer camp begins Monday, June 18!

What: Five days of hands on learning with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Who: Students entering 5-8th grade.

When: Monday, June 18 to Friday, June 22

Hours: 9am-12pm

Where: Holy Family Bilingual School, first floor mac lab. 9615 20th Ave SW, Seattle, WA 98106

Instructors: Ivette Cortez and Madeline Stevens. Both of whom have attended an immersive code school teaching JavaScript meant for creating websites.

Cost: $300 per student, for the week. Scholarships can be applied for by completing the application on this site! You can email either Ivette or Madeline with additional questions.

What to bring: Snacks for breaks. And a positive attitude!

Regular School Program

  • Explore and discover the technology behind building websites!
  • Students will learn the basics of web development and basic web design frameworks - HTML and CSS - through creation of their own static websites.
  • During the first 6 sessions, students will learn best practices through the agile workflow model used in tech companies from Amazon to Zulily.
  • During the last session, students will present their website as a final project.
  • Limited space - Only 12 spots available!

About the team

Ivette Cortez

A Chicago native, Ivette has dedicated most of her life to working with underrepresented populations. A first generation daughter of Guatemalan immigrants, she would spend her summers in Guatemala, where she developed a love of learning. In 2016, she learned front-end development and uses her skills to develop a programming curriculum at Holy Family Bilingual School. She is passionate about empowering women, girls, and promoting equity and diversity in tech and hopes to introduce programming to more generations of students in Seattle.


Madeline Stevens

Madeline previously worked as a multimedia journalist, a reading and writing tutor, a technician/trainer at Apple, a TA at the code school where her and Ivette met. Her goals are to continue working in the tech industry as a developer while also continuing to work with the kiddos.
